# Preservation M+ Guide

Preservation is a healing spec that offers an array of useful tools in M+. Burst healing, mobility, and unique utility allow it to bring strengths that differ from most healing specializations. Its healing style blends proactive and reactive elements, giving Preservation the flexibility to handle a wide range of damage profiles.

When it comes to how Preservation feels to play in M+, the spec revolves around layering multiple HoTs on the party in preparation for incoming damage. This is often followed by spreading Echo across party members and using a large healing ability such as !Verdant Embrace!, !Merithra’s Blessing!, or !Dream Breath! to quickly stabilize the group.

## Stats

For M+ we will be running Mastery > Haste > Versatility > Critical Strike

**Mastery **is our best stat for healing throughput. This stat increases our healing done to allies by our mastery percentage while their health is lower than our own.

**Haste **is our next best stat due to the way it interacts with our spells. It reduces the cooldown of many of our spells, as well as reduces our GCD. The more haste you have the more you will find that your echo ramps are faster, as well as !verdant embrace!, !temporal anomaly!, and !reversion! off cooldown more often. A very important side note is that haste changes the way the spec _feels_ to play, meaning some players may opt to play more haste in order to get their echoes out faster, or in general feel better about their cooldowns on certain spells.

**Versatility **is our third best stat purely for the fact that it is an increase to healing, damage, and damage reduction. It doesn’t have any special interactions besides simply making our numbers bigger.

**Critical Strike** isn’t a terrible stat, it has some good upsides such as helping our !Reversion!s last longer as well as !Dream Breath! And !Fire Breath! If we have !Double-Time! talent. The main reason critical strike falls behind the other three is due to it not affecting a decent amount of our healing. Things such as !Golden Hour!, !Temporal Anomaly!, and !Rewind! cannot crit, therefore get no value from the stat.





## Core Abilities

**!Echo! **is the most important ability to understand in our kit, it allows us to replicate our next non-essence healing spell on the target. Having multiple echoes out on the party at the same time allows us to transform a single target healing spell like Verdant Embrace into a huge aoe healing tool. 

**!Verdant Embrace!** is a large single target heal that we will be using as an aoe heal for the majority of the time. While being our burstiest heal in most situations, it is important to get really good use out of this spell, it can almost single handedly carry mechanics with the amount of healing it can do. An added bonus to this spell is !Lifebind!, which does not get echoed, meaning you only get one lifebind per !Verdant Embrace! cast so make sure to always target the party member you want to benefit from this before casting!

!**Reversion**! Is a very overloaded spell, it is a HoT, a damage reduction, our apex talent, a healing amplifier, a single target heal, and an aoe heal all in one. This spell has multiple passives baked in that make it the right button to push for many scenarios. Most of the time you will find yourself echoing reversion in order to keep up our apex talent damage reduction and our Grace Period healing amp. It will be used as a large amount of healing as well due to another passive, Golden Hour. Golden Hour makes Reversion a very strong instant heal on whoever recently took damage, and this effect can echo.

**!Merithra’s Blessing! **Is our apex talent, and is effectively just a better reversion. You get all the benefits of hitting reversion previously stated with an additional extra burst of healing. You can proc this ability by hitting an essence spender as well as casting Dream Breath. The guaranteed proc from Dream Breath will be something you plan on playing around often.

!**Temporal Anomaly**! Is basically a big ball of echoes, it is able to apply a weaker echo to the first 5 targets it hits. This spell is very good at supplying us with a consistent flow of healing depending on how we use the echoes it gives, as well as some very nice shielding that stacks up to three times if it is refreshed.

!**Dream Breath**! Is our first empowered spell. It can be ranked up to provide more instant healing with less duration on the HoT, but for most cases we will just be casting at rank 1.

!**Fire Breath! **Is our second empower ability, we get a lot of use out of this due to how it is most of our damage but also is an extremely useful healing ability. With talents like Life-giver’s Flame and Leaping Flames, this actually becomes one of our best heals we have to offer. 

!**Living Flame! **Is our filler spell, we really don’t want to be casting this for healing unless it is buffed by Lifespark or Leaping Flames. We also get to use this as our filler damage spell.

!**Emerald Blossom! **Is a very small aoe heal that we can push in very specific scenarios involving everyone being stacked and having nothing better to push.

Each of these spells have multiple interactions with each other that you can read up on the spell interactions page.




Healing Cooldowns

!**Tip the Scales!** Makes our next empowered spell an instant cast at max rank. For Flameshaper it isn’t much, but for Chronowarden it is a huge healing cooldown thanks to Temporal Burst. We will be planning heavily around this spell.

!**Stasis**! Is probably the most notorious part of preservation evoker that makes it “complicated” to new preservation players. It copies and stores the last three healing spells you pushed in that order, remembering who you targeted when you cast the spell. There are many tricks and combinations you can do to make this spell a very powerful cooldown that we will talk about later. Making good use of this spell will make or break your keys. It is a choose one option with !Dream Flight!, but we will always be taking Stasis

!**Rewind**! While very strong in raid it is a bit lack luster in keys. It is quite a strong burst of healing in a lot of situations, but its long cooldown makes it hard to really play around. Generally, this is a panic button that you can hit if something goes wrong, or you find yourself out of cooldowns to rotate through. This button is an extremely strong tank heal however, and can often be used simply to help keep your tank alive for 5 more seconds if they are spiking hard.

!**Zephyr**! Is a nice little group defensive that can be used to help smooth out damage if our healing is low, or keep someone alive from a one-shot. It is important to be up to date on what actually gets affected by this spell, as there are many cases where you think it should but it doesn’t and visaversa.

!**Time Dilation**! Is our external defensive cool down that we can use very liberally on DPS and very conservatively on Tanks. This defensive staggers damage over time meaning after the effect ends the target will be taking more damage than they are planning around, which is why pushing this on your tank at the wrong time might just end up killing them.

!**Obsidian Scales**! Is our personal defensive that we can use to keep ourselves healthy during damage. Hitting this button is extremely effective against damage due to the fact that we also get !Renewing Blaze! At the same time. While it isn’t strictly a healing cooldown normally, it is important to understand that the healthier we are, the healthier our group is. It is very common to understand that hitting your defensive during a damage event as a healer helps you focus more on the rest of the group or save other tools for later.. As flameshaper, this can be given to a party member at lower effectiveness as well when you activate it.

!**Cauterize! and !Naturalize**! Are two separate dispels that remove the effects listed, and they can also be put in stasis!




## Utility

!Time Spiral! Lets us give our party one extra use of their movement cooldown and really isn’t anything special in M+, but it can be pretty nice to use randomly for yourself to get an extra charge of !Hover!. 

!Spatial Paradox! Increases the range of all our spells. This can be useful when trying to pull mobs far away for our group or just lets us heal far away people in certain fights. Keep in mind that hitting Fire Breath while this is active will most likely pull the entire dungeon.

!Deep Breath! Is technically a damage button, but in almost every scenario it’s better used for the stun that you should always be taking.

!Oppressing Roar! Can be very nice if you are expecting your group to use their CCs, or you can just press it before you press !Deep Breath! To increase your own stun.

!Rescue! No longer gives a shield, but is still quite useful for removing roots and potentially some skips in the future. There have been effects in the past that deal damage or a heal absorb, that due to the fact that it comes with a slow, means rescue could completely remove the effect.

!Wing Buffet! And !Tail Swipe! Are our two racial CCs that knock up and knock back respectively, and are very useful as a pseudo interrupt in many cases if used well.

!Landslide! Is an aoe root that can be useful when mobs fixate or the group needs to kite. It will break pretty easily and keep in mind it will put you in combat, it is not functional to stop a patrol like a druid’s entangling roots.

!Sleep Walk! Is an extremely unique CC that works to move a mob out of its usual place. It will not pull any of the units nearby and can be used to do many interesting things. It is broken easily when damaged.

Healing Rotation

While Preservation doesn’t have a set rotation, there are many priorities to keep in mind while healing. Preservation is about making the most efficient use of all the tools it has, and getting the most uses out of our strongest abilities possible while making sure they are buffed by all the right passives.




## The Importance of Reversion

Reversion’s importance to our healing cannot be understated. Here is a list of all the things reversion does.

- 10% Healing per reversion on the target.
- 2% Damage Reduction and Heal Back per reversion on the target
- Has a chance to proc Essence Burst
- Instantly heals the target for 15% of damage taken in the last 5 seconds on cast
- Heals the target for a decent amount over time
- Healing done by the spell has a chance to proc Life Spark

Reversion is something we are actively putting out through the entire dungeon, using tools such as Echo and Temporal Anomaly to get more out faster. Even while out of combat we will be applying reversions so we can be prepared for when combat starts. 

So now that we have our reversions out, we want to keep them out. Most of the random instances of damage people will be taking are covered by Golden Hour and the ticking hots we have rolling on the party, so by healing our party with Reversion, we are also refreshing the duration of our Reversions.

Something to also point out about Reversion is that Merithra’s Blessing is basically an upgraded Reversion. When we push Merithra’s Blessing, we get all the benefits of reversion as well, with an additional burst heal. Any point in time that you were going to hit Reversion normally, it is perfectly fine to hit your Merithra’s Blessing instead.




## Echo Strength

A key factor to !Echo! Is that depending on how you apply the buff changes how strong the spell replication it gives is. This is called “Echo strength” and the base spell echo starts with a strength of 70%. 

There are multiple talents that change the strength of our echoes and it is important to understand that the base strength of our hardcasted echos and our echoes from Temporal Anomaly via Resonating Sphere are different. While a hardcasted Echo is 70% baseline, a Temporal Anomaly Echo starts out at a strength of 30%. With !Time Lord! We get a 50% increase to the strength of our echoes, which makes our hardcast Echo now 105% and our Temporal Anomaly Echo 45%. As we will always run !Time Lord! In m+, you can see that a replicated spell on hardcasted echo ends up being stronger than the initial cast. 

Additionally, being Chronowarden gives you a 10% increase to echo strength and if you choose to take it, !Titan’s Gift! Increases the echo strength of echoes you cast with essence burst by 35%. 




## Healing Flow

Now that we’ve explained the most important parts of understanding preservation, we have to learn how to use them. 

Firstly we want to get out reversions before damage happens, as we want to have all of our reversion benefits running beforehand. Additionally, if we know damage is imminent, we can cast a rank 1 Dream Breath to have a Merithra’s Blessing proc ready as well as the healing from Dream Breath rolling. 

With reversions and potentially Dream Breath out on our party we have to ask ourselves, “When is the next time someone in my group will take damage?” Usually, the answer is pretty easy depending on how familiar we are with the dungeon. With some reps in a dungeon, you will get used to when and what does damage that needs to be healed. 

A very good example of damage knowledge in a dungeon is healing a boss mechanic. A boss like Emberdawn in Windrunner Spire has very clear damage patterns. It starts out with two targets being chosen for the Flaming Updraft mechanic, which is a large amount of single target damage on the two allies who are targeted. 

Naturally we will want to heal them, and the best way to do this is to prepare early. As soon as we see our party members targeted by damage, we want to make sure they have echoes on them before we need to heal them. Echo on its own is a very weak global, and is only made strong by the spell we cast after. This makes a lot of our preparation for damage casting a lot of echoes on full health allies. 

With rolling reversions and prepared echos sitting on our target waiting to be consumed, the moment our allies take damage we want to cast Verdant Embrace, Reversion, or Dream Breath.

Verdant Embrace is our favorite option, it does the most healing and we will always want to default to pushing the first if it’s available. Reversion is another really good option in this case, as it excels at single target healing through Golden Hour which is also echoed. Dream Breath doesn’t do a ton of healing, but it does give us a Merithra’s Proc that we can also echo directly after. 

After consuming our Echoes with one of our options, our allies will be healed a large amount. In most cases however, they will continue taking damage. To continue healing, we can look at a few choices. 

We have already used our Verdant Embrace and won’t have it back for a while, so we must look at our next two best options. Due to the fact that we either have Dream Breath off cooldown, or previously cast it before the damage to have it rolling, we should have a guaranteed Merithra’s in our hands. 

If it’s off cooldown, I strongly believe that sending the rank 1 Dream Breath now in order to proc our Merithra’s is the correct choice if we didn’t do it beforehand. 

With Merithra’s at the ready, it is generally the next best heal in the priority to make use of after Verdant Embrace. We can use this Merithra’s in this situation by echoing one (or both if we have the time) and casting Merithra’s. 

If they are still taking damage after using Verdant Embrace, Dream Breath, and our guaranteed Meritha’s proc, we can finally move on to just cast echo and reversion. Casting Echo on both teammates affected and pressing reversion will do startling amounts of healing and will definitely be enough to get them through the last bit of the debuff. 

After successfully healing the mechanic, we want to go back to step one, make sure we have plenty of Reversions out for the next damage event, and start ramping our Echoes again.
